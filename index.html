Project Overview
Project Context and Justification

During my internship at Truck Support Venlo, I was tasked with addressing a significant challenge that impacts the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of their operations—tire procurement for trucks. Unlike the car tire sector, which benefits from a variety of advanced analytical tools, the truck tire sector suffers from a noticeable gap in similar technologies. This disparity has left warehouse managers at Truck Support without the necessary tools to make informed purchasing decisions, relying on fragmented and often outdated data to compare tire prices and quality.

Project Goal

The primary goal of this project to develop a straightforward yet powerful software tool designed to streamline the tire buying process for warehouse managers. By providing a comprehensive comparison of tire suppliers along with actionable buying advice, the software aims to ensure that warehouse managers can procure high-quality tires at the best possible prices. This, in turn, enhances operational efficiency and drives cost savings across the organization.

Project Scope

The project scope was clearly defined to focus exclusively on the procurement of truck tires. Key features included:

  • Market Research: Conducting extensive market research to gather accurate and current data on tire prices and supplier reliability.
  • Scalable Application: Developing a scalable application that could adapt to growing data and user needs without degradation in performance.
  • Development of Scraping Bots: Creating sophisticated web scraping bots that automate the collection of tire data from various online sources, ensuring the data is current and comprehensive.

Conversely, the project was scoped with specific exclusions to maintain focus and manage resources effectively:

  • The application would not support the procurement of other types of goods or services.
  • It would not facilitate direct negotiations or transactions between warehouse managers and suppliers.
  • The focus would remain on suppliers within a specific region or country, excluding global market data unless relevant and feasible.
Constraints and Conditions

The development of the project was underpinned by several conditions:

  • A research paper would be prepared to document the findings and methodologies used.
  • An application architecture that includes both frontend and backend components would be developed, along with a working demo.
  • Technical assistance would be available as needed to address any complex challenges that might arise during development.

Project Methodology

Agile ScrumBan Methodology

I used a method called Agile ScrumBan for the project. It mixes planning and flexibility, allowing me to adjust quickly to any new demands or changes. This method helped me keep improving the product through regular updates based on feedback from the users.

Tools and Technologies

I chose modern and efficient tools and technologies to ensure robust development and scalability of the solution:

Docker was pivotal in ensuring that all backend components, like the SQL database, my Flask application, and the n8n workflow automation, were seamlessly integrated and could be deployed consistently across various environments. This containerization significantly streamlined my development and deployment processes, allowing for rapid scalability and easy updates.

MySQL is used as the relational database to manage and store all project data efficiently, ensuring high performance and scalability, critical for handling complex data structures and large volumes of information.

I used Python, enhanced with a tool called Selenium, to collect data from different websites. This helps keep our app's information up to date automatically.

n8n is a tool that I use to organize the flow of data from my data collection to the database. It ensures that the data is processed and stored correctly and efficiently.

FlutterFlow was chosen for its fast development capabilities and performance efficiency, used by me to develop a responsive and user-friendly interface for the dashboard.

Research and Analysis

Data Collection for Dashboard Development

To develop a comprehensive and user-friendly dashboard, I first tackled the challenge of collecting truck tire price data from diverse sources like websites, emails, and supplier databases. This required effective web scraping techniques and a strategy for managing different data formats and access restrictions.

Data Collection and Normalization

I used Python and libraries such as BeautifulSoup and Selenium to automate data collection, ensuring accuracy and timeliness. Data normalization processes were implemented to convert all measurements to a consistent format, such as converting rim diameters from inches to millimeters and standardizing manufacturer names across datasets.

Legal and Ethical Considerations in Data Collection

It was imperative to adhere to legal and ethical standards in web scraping. I established guidelines to ensure compliance with laws and ethical norms, focusing on permissible sources and methods of data extraction.

System Architecture and Technology Optimization

The overall architecture was designed to support scalability and quick data retrieval. MySQL was used for the database to efficiently store and manage the vast amount of collected data. For the dashboard development, I selected React for its responsiveness and ability to create an interactive user interface tailored to the needs of warehouse managers at Truck Support Venlo.

Results and Impact

The implementation of these methods resulted in a dynamic dashboard that significantly improves the efficiency of purchasing decisions by providing clear, actionable insights into tire pricing and availability. This solution has enabled Truck Support Venlo to make cost-effective purchases and better manage their inventory.

Challenges and Future Directions

Challenges such as dealing with inconsistent data sources and integrating new supplier information were met with ongoing adjustments to scraping scripts and data handling procedures. Future work will focus on expanding the dashboard’s features and improving its data processing capabilities to include real-time updates and predictive analytics.

Development Process

Prototyping and Stakeholder Feedback

Initially, I used Figma to create detailed prototypes of the application. This allowed me to present visual representations to stakeholders, ensuring that their requirements were clearly understood and met. This step was crucial for validating the project scope and the user interface before moving into actual development.

System Architecture Design

After confirming the design and requirements, I developed the architecture of the application, which included creating context diagrams, database schemas, and Docker container configurations. These diagrams helped in visualizing the structure of the application and the interactions between different components, ensuring all parts were well integrated and scalable.

Implementation and Continuous Integration

The development was hosted on GitLab, where I leveraged CI/CD pipelines to automate builds, tests, and deployments, enhancing the development workflow and ensuring consistency across environments. This setup supported non-functional requirements like security, performance, and maintainability.

Data Collection and Normalization

To populate the application with real-world data, I built scraping scripts to automate the collection of tire data from various online sources. This data was then processed using Flask to handle API requests and n8n to normalize and format the data before sending it to the database.

Frontend Development with FlutterFlow

For the frontend, I utilized FlutterFlow to build a dynamic and responsive user interface. This tool allowed me to quickly fetch and display data from the backend, offering stakeholders a functional and interactive dashboard that met their operational needs.
